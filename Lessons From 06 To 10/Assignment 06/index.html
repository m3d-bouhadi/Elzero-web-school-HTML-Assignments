Novels Section

Animal Farm

Animal Farm by George Orwell is a political allegory that satirizes the Russian Revolution and the subsequent rise of Stalinism. It depicts a group of farm animals who overthrow their human farmer, only to face tyranny under their own kind.

1984

1984 by George Orwell: This dystopian novel explores a totalitarian regime led by Big Brother, where surveillance and propaganda control every aspect of citizens' lives. It delves into themes of oppression, truth, and the manipulation of reality.

Brave New World

Brave New World by Aldous Huxley: Set in a future society where technological advancements and state control create a superficially perfect but emotionally barren world. It examines the consequences of a consumerist culture that sacrifices individuality and human connection for stability and pleasure.
